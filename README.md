# Egalitarian_Social_Policing

Supplementary Materials associated with the Netlogo model for the submitted MS: ‘Modelling egalitarian social policing of prestige-based status hierarchies in human social evolution', by Ben Gleeson (Fenner School, Australian National University).

This model examines evolved human socio-behavioural dynamics with a view to explaining paradoxically high levels of contemporary male violence and social status. It is based on a conceptual synthesis of ‘the challenge hypothesis’ (Wingfield, 2017; Wingfield et al., 1990) and ‘biosocial model’ hypothesis (Mazur, 1985; Mazur & Booth, 1998) of testosterone, together with theorised ‘social selection’ against domineering ancestral males (Boehm, 1993, 2017; Gintis et al., 2019; Knauft, 1991; Sarkar & Wrangham, 2023; Wrangham, 2021a, 2021b), and inferred transitions from dominance-based male status competition to complex and socially-mediated ‘prestige-based’ forms (Erdal & Whiten, 1994; Henrich & Gil-White, 2001; Zeng et al., 2021). It depicts a generic hominin social environment where social status competition allows success in culturally constructed group hierarchies, but impulsive (‘reactive’) aggression is punished.

The model itself may be accessed and run by either downloading the <a href="https://github.com/Ben-Gleeson71/Egalitarian_Social_Policing/blob/main/Gleeson_Supplementary3_nlogoxmodel.nlogox">.nlogox</a> file and opening with an installed copy of <a href="https://www.netlogo.org/">Netlogo</a>, or via <a href="https://netlogoweb.org/">Netlogo Web</a> by downloading and opening the provided <a href="https://github.com/Ben-Gleeson71/Egalitarian_Social_Policing/blob/main/Gleeson_Supplementary4_HTMLmodel.html">HTML version</a> of the model.

The basic form of the model allows the user to select a population size and average reactive aggression, click 'set up' and 'go', then monitor aspects of agent social interaction, including Maximum group size, Max Status-level, Mean Status-level, and to see graphed Status-level distributions. With the 'Sex-bimodal?' switch set to 'on', the user may also select different ratios of Female to Male reactive aggression (RA) and Status Score (SS). Adjusting the 'F:M-Reactive-Aggression' slider determines the mean of the normal distribution of female Reactivity by applying the selected percentage ratio of the set ‘Mean-RA’ for that population. The 'F:M-Status-Score' slider determines the mean of the normal distribution of female SS by applying the selected percentage ratio to the population mean SS. While trying various settings, the user can monitor the percentages of Male and Female agents that are members of groups as opposed to wandering defectors, and the relative Status-level of Male and Female agents.

More information is available in the model 'Info' tab at top centre of the open model environment and in the provided supplementary information (i.e., the <a href="https://github.com/Ben-Gleeson71/Egalitarian_Social_Policing/blob/main/Gleeson_Supplementary1_ODD.docx">ODD protocol</a>  and <a href="https://github.com/Ben-Gleeson71/Egalitarian_Social_Policing/blob/main/Gleeson_Supplementary2_Overview.docx">Language-based description</a> docs).
